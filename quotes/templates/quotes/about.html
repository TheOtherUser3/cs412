    Joe Biden


    Joseph Robinette Biden Jr. (born November 20, 1942) is an American politician who served as the 46th President of the United States from 2021-2025. A member of the Democratic Party, he served as the 47th vice president from 2009 to 2017 under Barack Obama and represented Delaware in the U.S. Senate from 1973 to 2009. He is notable for having run for president in the 1988 and 2008 elections before winning the presidency in 2020. While having initial legislative success as Presient, his administration was largely viewed as a failure due to high inflation, rising gas prices, and a variety of foreign policy crises, including the chaotic withdrawal from Afghanistan and his controversial response to the Israel-Hamas War, started on October 7th. His Presidency was plagued with criticism of his age and mental acuity, with many believing that he was not mentally fit to be president, although that claim has never been substantiated. He is now most notable for having failed to accomplish his goal of stopping Donald Trump, as his 2024 re-election bid is heavily blamed for having ruined the Democrats' chances of defeating Trump in 2024. Even after he dropped out of the race in July, 2024, over criticism of his age and mental acuity, his VP and heir, Kamala Harris, could not recover from the hole Biden's bid left them in, and she ended up losing the election to Trump. Trump has now rolled back most of Biden's agenda, and thus Biden is largely viewed as a failed president.
